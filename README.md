# Coffee Orders Sales Analysis Dashboard

## Business Problem
A coffee retail business wants to analyze its sales performance, customer purchasing behavior, and product trends to make data-driven business decisions.

---

## Objective
The objective of this project was to clean, transform, and analyze coffee sales data and build an interactive Excel dashboard to track key sales metrics and customer trends.

---

## Dataset
The dataset contains:
- Customer information
- Product details
- Order transactions

---

## Data Cleaning & Transformation
Performed the following operations using Microsoft Excel:

- Populated missing columns in Orders table using XLOOKUP
- Standardized product size formatting
- Converted currency values into dollars
- Formatted date columns
- Created derived columns such as Roast Type Name
- Structured raw data for analysis

---

## Dashboard Features
Built an interactive dashboard using:
- Pivot Tables
- Pivot Charts
- Timeline filters
- Slicers

---

## Key KPIs Analyzed
- Total Sales over time
- Top 5 customers
- Sales by Country
- Year on Year growth %
- Year to Date/Cumulative sales

---

## Business Insights
- Arabica and Liberica Drive Peak Revenue
- United States has the highest sales
- Allis Willmore is the top customer
- The time-series data shows extreme "peaks and valleys" rather than a steady growth trend or clear seasonal stability.
- Most YoY growth lines are dipping toward the 0% or negative (below 200.00% axis) mark as of mid-2022. This indicates that the explosive growth seen in 2021 is stabilizing.
---

## Project Outcome/Suggestions/Way Forward
- While the US is the clear stronghold, the business is highly vulnerable to US market fluctuations.
- There is a significant opportunity to investigate why the UK market is underperforming and potentially scale marketing efforts in Ireland, which shows a decent secondary foothold.
- High-inventory levels should be maintained for Arabica & Liberica types during historical peak months (like September and January) to ensure demand is met.
- The business is entering a "maturity" phase where it can no longer rely on easy YoY gains and may need to innovate or find new markets to restart the growth engine.
